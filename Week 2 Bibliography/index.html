History

The inception of the team

The Royals entered the American League in 1969 along with the Seattle Pilots. They were founded by Kansas City businessman Ewing Kauffman.

The team was established after Stuart Symington, then-U.S. Senator from Missouri, demanded a new franchise for the city after the Athletics moved to Oakland in 1968.

Since 1973, the Royals have played at Kauffman Stadium.

The name

In 1968, the team established a contest to name the team that received more than 17,000 ideas.

A bridge engineer named Sanford Porte from Overland Park, Kansas, won the contest with “Royals” entry.

The name actually originates from Missouri’s billion-dollar livestock income, as well as Kansas City being the nation’s leading stocker and feeder market and the home of the American Royal parade and pageant.

World Series Wins

1986

The Royals originally fell behind 1-3 in the series against their rival St. Louis Cardinals. That's right, the Cavs weren't the first to do it.

The team won 6-1 in game 5, and the two teams headed back to Kansas City for game six.

In game seven, Bret Saberhagen shutout the Cardinals as Kansas City won 11–0, clinching their first World Series title.

2015

After getting a lucky wildcard entry in 2014, the Royals won their first division title since 1985 and first Central division title ever.

The Royals bested the Houston Astros in the ALDS, and defeated the Toronto Blue Jays in Game 6 to win the 2015 ALCS and reach their fourth World Series.

They beat the New York Mets in just 5 games to become the 2015 World Series champions. It was the Royals' first World Series title since 1985, 30 years later.
